Week 1

Personal Learning Experiences

Experience 1

A memory you recall from when you were learning something in elementary or middle school
Learning the tables is definitely a memory I cannot forget so easily. This was something very challenging for me since the way I was thought to learn multiplication tables was by memorizing them. As an adult, I came to realize that there are many other ways that are easier and more effective to dominate this topic.

Experience 2

A memory from when you learned something in high school or college
There was a biology presentation I was getting prepared to do the following day. So I collected all information regarding these topics and jotted down the essential information. At the very least, I came across a video on YouTube related to the presentation topics. The funny thing is, the name of the video was not even close to any of the topics the teacher had provided to research, yet, on the day of the presentation she asked a question regarding it. For some reason, the video and its content had stuck in my brain and I could remember every detail clearly and succeed at my presentation.

Experience 3

An experience you had learning in a professional setting (first job, current career, etc.).
When I began a new position at a job, the company had recently decided to switch to a different technology to create the learning material. There was various documentation of the processes that each ID needs to go through in order to create these learning materials, however, since this was a new technology about to be used, there was a need to create new documentation for the new technology.
I was delegated to carry out the majority of this task and had to learn how to create a detailed and highly outlined, very organized, visually appealing, and high-level readability document. Of course, I used various of the previous document to gain vision and context as to how these documents are usually created and successfully got the task done.
