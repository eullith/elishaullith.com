Week 6

Authentic Assessment

The learning scenario will focus on learning Spanish, a popular and widely spoken language around the world. Students will start by learning the basics of the language, such as pronunciation, grammar, and vocabulary. They will be introduced to common phrases and expressions used in daily conversation, which they will practice through various activities and exercises.

Description

The authentic assessment for this learning scenario will be a project in which students are required to demonstrate their language proficiency by producing an audio or video recording of themselves engaging in a conversation in Spanish with a native speaker. The project will involve students first learning the basics of the language, including grammar rules, vocabulary, and common phrases. They will then engage in guided practice activities, such as dialogue and role-playing exercises, to build their language proficiency. Finally, students will record themselves engaging in a conversation with a native Spanish speaker, demonstrating their ability to use the language effectively.
This assessment is student-centered and authentic because it focuses on the practical application of the language. By engaging in a conversation with a native speaker, students will have the opportunity to apply what they have learned in a real-world setting. This assessment also emphasizes the importance of communication skills, which are essential for success in the global marketplace.

Connections to Learning Theory

The cognitivist approach to learning emphasizes that learners process information and acquire knowledge through mental processes such as attention, perception, memory, and problem-solving. In this assessment, students will be required to apply these mental processes to learn and master a new language. Students will need to attend to the language instruction, perceive the different sounds and patterns of the language, use memory to recall the new vocabulary and grammar rules, and problem-solve to apply this knowledge to a real-life conversation.
Furthermore, the project emphasizes the importance of meaningful learning, which is another key aspect of cognitivism. By engaging in a conversation with a native speaker, students will be able to see the relevance of their learning and how it can be applied in a practical way. This project also provides opportunities for self-regulated learning, as students will be required to set goals, monitor their progress, and adjust their learning strategies accordingly.
